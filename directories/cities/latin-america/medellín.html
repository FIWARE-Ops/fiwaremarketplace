Surrounded by mountains and beautiful nature, Medellín is situated in the Cordillera Central, in the Andes. It is the secondlargest city in Colombia, with over 2.5 million inhabitants. It has an area of 105 km2 of urban land, 270 km2 of rural land, and 5.2 km2 for potential further expansion. It stands out as one of Colombia’s main financial, industrial, commercial, and service centres.

Medellín is a Special District of Science, Technology, and Innovation. In 2020, it began the transformation from being the industrial capital of the country to becoming the Software Valley of Colombia. To achieve this, hundreds of activities are being developed that use the technologies of the Fourth Industrial Revolution and the digital economy to improve its inhabitants’ quality of life.
