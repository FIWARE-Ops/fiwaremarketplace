The development and maintenance of a healthy living environment are therefore of crucial importance for the health and wellbeing of the people.

One specific area that impacts healthy urban living is mobility and air quality. Although people in The Netherlands cycle a lot, there is a shortage in valuable and usable data about cycling compared to car traffic. As a result, cycling is often underexposed in mobility policies.

The Utrecht Region wants to be a leader with regards to cycle knowledge, data, and tools. Its strategy is about “Healthy Urban Living” and it contributes to that with various FIWARE-related projects, also including EV-charging stations (charging station data and parking sensors to provide better information about usage), the IRISproject (energy transition) and their open data portal.
